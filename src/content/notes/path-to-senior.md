For two years I treated Senior like a checklist. Ship more features. Mentor more juniors. Get more visibility. The checklist was real, and I made progress, and the title did not move.

What actually shifted things: picking the problems nobody else wanted to own, then owning them end to end. Not the hero stuff. The unglamorous "this view is slow and confusing and nobody knows why." Take it from "someone should fix this" to "here is the new pattern we'll use across the codebase."

Senior is not what you do. It's what you decide to own.
