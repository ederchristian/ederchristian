The hard part was never the time zone. It was the absence of corridor talk. The half-jokes that turn into a project. The "while I'm here" decisions that never get written down.

I solve it the way most remote engineers eventually do: write more, faster, and earlier. Decisions live in PRs, not in calls. Calls are for unblocking, not for thinking.

The good part is the other 22 hours of the day. Walks, lunch with my wife, BJJ. The kind of life a Manhattan salary used to pay for as compensation.
